Will the CLARITY Act Be Signed Into Law Before August?

A probability estimate for the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (H.R. 3633) reaching the President's desk and being signed on or before 31 July 2026.

TON618 Capital Research · Prepared under CFA Institute analytical standards · For information purposes only

~6%Central estimate
We estimate a ~6% probability (plausible range 3–14%) that the CLARITY Act is signed into law on or before 31 July 2026. The bill is real, advancing, and has a credible 2026 path — the prediction market prices a 42.5% chance it is signed at some point this year — but the before-August window is the binding constraint. Three sequential gates (a 60-vote Senate floor passage, House concurrence in the Senate text, and the signature) must all clear inside roughly 15 Senate session days that are split by the 27 Jun–12 Jul recess, with two substantive disputes still unresolved. The base case is that market-structure law is an H2-2026 story, not a July one.

Conclusion first: a sub-August signing is a low-probability near-term catalyst. The larger, better-priced catalyst window opens after Labor Day (Senate returns 14 Sep), ahead of the midterm campaign recess.

We believe the three things that matter for a sub-August signing are:

  • The seven-Democrat gate. Sixty votes are needed to invoke cloture. Republicans hold ~53 seats; only 2 Democrats backed the bill in committee. Leadership must find ~7 Democrats on the floor while holding its own caucus (e.g., members with stablecoin-competition or ethics concerns).
  • Two unresolved disputes. The stablecoin-yield question (the White House's 1 March compromise deadline lapsed without published text) and the Van Hollen ethics / conflict-of-interest amendment (failed 11–13 in committee) are both heading to the floor unsettled. A floor vote is unlikely to be called until a path on both exists.
  • Floor time and the calendar. Majority Leader Thune controls a midterm-year floor crowded with appropriations and nominations. Sen. Lummis has placed the "probable" floor window before the August recess; Sen. Hagerty's hope for a pre–July 4 vote is now off the table given the 27 Jun–12 Jul recess.

3Method: a three-source ensemble

We triangulate three independent estimators rather than rely on any single one, then gate the result on the legislative calendar.

  1. Structural gate model (primary). Decompose the remaining path into sequential conditional probabilities and multiply. This is auditable and isolates the binding constraint.
  2. Market-implied anchor (cross-check). Polymarket's "signed in 2026" contract (42.5% Yes, $1.4m volume) is a money-weighted full-year estimate; we convert it to a before-August figure using a timing split.
  3. Precedent base rate (sanity band). The GENIUS Act is the closest comparable: Senate passage → House concurrence → signature in ~1 month (Senate 68–30 on 17 Jun 2025; signed 18 Jul 2025). But it cleared the Senate comfortably and the House adopted the Senate text wholesale — neither condition is yet met for CLARITY.

4The conditional-gate model

P(signed ≤ 31 Jul) = P(Senate passes ≥60 by ~28 Jul) × P(House clears the Senate text by 31 Jul | Senate passed) × P(President signs by 31 Jul | both chambers passed). The ~28 Jul sub-deadline leaves a few days for House action and signature inside the window.

The full-year market prices 42.5%. 7Investment relevance

For a Bitcoin-benchmarked book, CLARITY is a sentiment- and flow-relevant catalyst (regulatory clarity for digital-commodity spot markets under the CFTC), but the timing of the catalyst is what is tradeable here. We believe the practical read is:

  • Do not pay up for a pre-August signing. At ~6%, the near-term event is unlikely; structuring exposure around a July signature has poor expected value.
  • The real catalyst window is post–Labor Day. The bulk of the market-implied 42.5% sits in the 14 Sep–2 Oct window and the post-election lame duck — both with more session days and, by then, possibly resolved disputes.
  • Mind "buy the rumor, sell the news." With the full-year probability already near a coin-flip and partially priced into crypto sentiment, the marginal upside on an actual signing may be muted; the larger asymmetric move could be to the downside on a clear failure signal (e.g., the bill slipping to 2027).
