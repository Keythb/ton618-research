Coinbase (COIN): The Right Company at the Wrong Price

A full valuation of Coinbase (COIN) mid-crypto-winter. The company is two businesses — a hyper-cyclical retail trading franchise and a growing "financial plumbing" annuity (the USDC stablecoin share, ETF custody, Deribit derivatives) that kept growing through the bear. Valued on normalized through-cycle earnings across weighted scenarios, fair value is ≈$102/share (range $85–$120) against a $165 market price — today's price treats a peak year as the permanent norm, and in every modeled scenario the stock returns less than simply holding bitcoin. Four charts, a reverse valuation of what $165 requires, a full Coinbase-buys-Circle analysis (≈12% odds in two years, with the August 2026 revenue-share renewal as the tell), a dated catalyst calendar including the live CLARITY Act floor vote, and the ten open questions on the name answered against the model. Macro Research · 30 June 2026

The Debt Endgame

How the United States most likely resolves a debt it cannot grow, default, or hike its way out of — through financial repression and stealth monetization, the inflation tax that shrinks the debt while widening the divide and raising popular misery. A new Fed chair, Kevin Warsh, inherits the trap; the market's favorite escape hatch — AI-driven deflation — is oversold, and even if real is captured by the Treasury rather than shared with households. On the merits, progressive wealth-transfer taxation (focused where the gains actually went) paired with growth-unlocking antitrust is the least-bad resolution — though the honest answer is the throttled one and the worst answer, inflation, needs no one's permission. Draws the post-WWII parallel (same repression lever, missing the growth engine), quantifies how stablecoins export the debasement abroad, and tests the wealth-inequality endgame — redistribute or rupture — stakeholder by stakeholder. Independently fact-checked in three adversarial passes against primary sources.

Thematic Research · 24 June 2026

How Close Is Quantum Computing to Being Useful?

A plain-English map of where quantum computing actually stands and an honest estimate of its pacing. The foundation milestone — error correction below threshold — was crossed in 2024–25, yet no machine has done anything economically useful a classical computer can't. "Useful" runs on three clocks: narrow scientific value now through 2030; broad commercial value in the 2030s; and a code-breaking machine that experts now put at roughly one-in-three odds within ten years — which is why banks, governments, and Bitcoin are already migrating off today's encryption. Six charts, an investment coda, and the Bitcoin-specific risk in proportion.
